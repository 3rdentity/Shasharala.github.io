     > i am an avid geek that spends a large majority of its time doing things it would rather not, particularly when there are so many interesting things going on and to be done. currently, most of that time is spent assisting customers of a fairly large electronic merchandise reseller navigate their way to making purchases. fortunately, i'm able to buy food, pay rent, and many other things by performing favorably for the electronic reseller, albeit, many of these tasks do not entertain me
     > when i'm not spending my time doing things i do not enjoy, i am probably sleeping or finding ways to manipulate things. manipulation involves coding and scripting, game design, website design, and even playing video games. i do not do much else. contemplating, reading, and writing are amusing tasks to me but tend to be lengthy endeavors that are not done in short instances and that i have very little time available for. video games tend to fall into this category for me as well. sometimes i consider this unfortunate and a waste of my time. i then endeavor to do something that i consider 'important'... or at least something that will impact the reality that a majority of humans tend to agree upon in a way that benefits me

     > usually this will involve staying up late studying computer science or putting in lots of extra hours at the electronic reseller i do work for. often this does not last very long before i start asking myself what i am doing exactly and why. i have quite a lot of derealization and often wonder what reasons there are to accept things as they currently are. even simple things like, "why am i still typing about myself"? as a result of this i also have some depersonalization. i often find myself tiring of simply existing. this will come back to me working very hard for a time to make my existence worthwhile before again wondering why i am working so much when i will eventually die.
     > while i find death fascinating and spend a considerable amount of time calculating its implications, i eventually get depressed or moody and do not want to see anyone or do anything. i will spend my time thinking about time. the ticking seconds of this existence measured in abstraction. the whirlwind of thoughts that insulate me inside a clockwork body, moving faster than i can manage to keep track of or begin to understand. the dizzying and overwhelming trek to find out what or who i am.
