     > i am an avid geek that spends a large majority of its time doing things it would rather not, particularly when there are so many interesting things going on and to be done. currently, a large portion of my time is spent assisting customers of a fairly large electronic merchandise reseller navigate their way to making purchases. fortunately, i'm able to buy food, pay rent, and many other things by performing favorably for the electronic reseller, albeit, many of these tasks do not entertain me

     > when i am not spending my time doing things i do not particularly enjoy, i am probably sleeping or finding ways to manipulate things. maybe i eat things sometimes. manipulation involves coding and scripting, game design, website design, and even playing video games. while i enjoy simply contemplating, reading, and writing things i feel like i have very little time available to do so. often i find the restraints of time are something that stresses me out a lot. i feel like there is a lot to do to keep reaching some unattainable goal, to get ahead of all the other humans near me, or to be something better. unfortunately, doing things like making video games and playing video games is not seen as a good career choice by many so i often question myself and my goals

     > sometimes these questions start to nag me enough that try to focus on doing something that most consider worthwhile. usually this will involve staying up late studying various things and completing certain tasks, like finishing something for this website or another website i work on. sometimes i put in lots of extra hours at the electronic reseller i work for. often this does not last very long before i start asking myself what i am doing exactly and why. i 'suffer' from a vague sense of derealization and often wonder what the meaning is of the things i do. i often find myself tiring of existing.
