  With the end of LD31 it is time to move on to other projects. Or, in this case, complete the unfinished entry I started for LD31. At this moment, my "game" has no gameplay features and freezes on startup. While it does render a single character/sprite to the screen the player has no way to interact with the game, since it crashes/halts silently. In other words, I did not finish my game.

  What stopped me from making a "complete" entry? In part it had to do with my inexperience with game design but it also had to do with making a collision detection system for the first time during the jam. Probably not my best idea. If I had not been so focused on making collision work, something plenty of games lack, I imagine I could have submitted something.

  Regardless, I intend to finish my spatial partitioning system and get clipping working in my game! In an effort to get myself to focus I am starting the One Game A Week challenge that Rami Ismail posited earlier this year. While actually creating entire games from scratch every week sounds difficult I feel that I can do it.

  I know there are going to be some really awful games I am going to make. They will be broken, will not play, or be distinctly ugly. I will keep trying though. And learning. That is what this is about. Dedicating myself and figuring out what works and what does not within a timeframe and in general. It will also force me to move on from projects that are starting to stagnate to ideas I am letting sit and rot.

  Outside of game creation I intend to start studying back back-end web development a bit more. It has been something that I have continually put off in favor of fiddling about with this website, some cool new open-source program, or Javascript, but I really want to get into it and build some awesome websites.

I will post regular updates here about my progress so if you are interested you may follow my Atom or RSS feeds for regular updates.
