  I've been pretty busy as of late. I've been looking for a new car, considering moving to a different state, and cramming lots and lots of Javascript patterns into my head. Most of those patterns have been focused around game development and the HTML5 Canvas element.

  In regards to getting a vehicle, I've mostly been perusing Craigslist, albeit half-heartedly. While I've seen a few vehicles that I have been interested in, somehow I find myself not making the necessary calls to check the vehicle out. Mostly it's a lack of general enthusiasm for anything and my stern focus on learning more front-end web development.

  Which I've actually been making plenty of progress in. I've gotten a great handle on jQuery, especially after Ludum Dare 30 and the HTML5 Canvas element is not nearly as scary as it had seemed a few months ago. While I would love to be focusing on WebGL and expanding my knowledge on back-end development, I'm still studying game loops and other bits and bobs of Javascript. Which is lots of fun! I just worry about not having forever to play with Javascript.

  Eventually, ideally, I'd like to read about PHP and SQL, learn to set up a web server, and live somewhere much colder. That's what the lingering moving thoughts are about. See, I'm not a fan of cities and I really dislike the sun. Unfortunately, the state of California has plenty of areas combining both of those elements. As a sort of lazy dream I've taken to mumbling and wistfully thinking about moving up to Washington or Oregon, but have yet to actually read anything about those areas. Not to mention how I will get to those areas, secure work, and find a place to rest my head without too much bother.

  I suppose that I will sort it all out eventually, but in the meantime I find it particularly stressful and tiring. I'm constantly tired it seems. I've just got to keep focusing.
