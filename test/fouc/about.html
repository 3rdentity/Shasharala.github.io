  I am a developer that is passionate about emerging niches in web and game design. I like to take a personal approach to my projects, resulting in an ardent obsession with my work. While some may think this is a negative trait, I try to use it to my advantage. I find a project more entertaining and care more when I am creating the things I dream about and envision. In response, my work flowers.

  When my focus wanders, as focus is wont to do, I am often reading something related to design and development. Usually, this is because I'm trying to learn how to do something new or I simply find the subject interesting. I'm like an odd sort of data sponge, constantly teaching myself new tricks, which I use to evaluate my personal standards. Outside of reading I tend to fiddle about with various video games and open source programs, spending substantial time enjoying the subtle nuances that make each of them function.

  While ruminating on interaction as a model for design, as I am wont to do, I prefer to drink tea and to be referred to with 'they'/'them' pronouns. When not working on a project, sipping hot leaf juice, or learning something new, I am unfortunately met with a pressing amount of anxiety. This reinforces my desire, my obsession with progression. Creating is just such a joy.

  Despite being an anxious introvert at heart, I do appreciate collaborating and communicating with others about coding, design, and various philosophies. I take part in a few game jams regularly, most notably Ludum Dare, and find those communities wonderfully supportive. In short, and after great length, I'm a human developer that tends to ramble, tries to focus, and often gets distracted.

-shasharala A.K.A. Ryan Combes
